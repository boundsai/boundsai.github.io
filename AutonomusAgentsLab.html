Research Focus

At the Autonomous Agents Lab, we are pioneering the future of robotics and autonomous systems. Leveraging advanced sensor technologies like 3D LiDAR, thermal imaging, millimeter-wave radar, and depth sensing, we create intelligent systems that navigate and interact seamlessly with their environments. Our high-performance computing hardware ensures these systems operate with unparalleled efficiency and precision.

Currently, we are exploring the potential of autonomous racing with our cutting-edge F1TENTH system, with plans to expand next year by acquiring a diverse array of robotic platforms. These will include teleoperation arms, agile robotic dogs, versatile industrial robots, innovative drones, and autonomous ground and sea vehicles. We aim to revolutionize fields such as environmental monitoring, search and rescue, autonomous transportation, and industrial automation. By integrating these technologies, we are developing innovative solutions that enhance human capabilities, tackle complex challenges, and improve quality of life in dynamic and impactful ways.

Ongoing Projects

Selected projects and initiatives at AA LAB.

  • Multi-modal End-to-end All-Weather Autonomous Driving

    (“Çoklu-modalite Uçtan-uca Tüm Hava Koşullarında Otonom Sürüş”)

    This work proposes an AI-based driving algorithm enhanced with a sensor-fusion network. The algorithm uses reliable sensor signals even when the available data are noisy or limited, enabling safer driving. It is designed to handle challenging traffic situations such as merging lanes, lane changes, and right-of-way decisions at intersections.

    • Funding: TÜBİTAK BİLGEM
    • Start: 03.06.2024
    • Estimated End: 30.05.2025
    • Status: Completed

  • F1TENTH Autonomous Racing Competition

    We explore high-speed autonomy and robust perception/control in constrained racing environments using the F1TENTH platform, focusing on real-time decision making, safety, and performance under uncertainty.

  • Robust Dense Correspondence via Multi-Modal Fusion and Transformers

    (“Çok Modlu Birleştirme ve Transformer’lar ile Sağlam Yoğun Eşleştirme”)

    Dense correspondence estimation between images is a fundamental component for computer vision tasks such as structure-from-motion, multi-view stereo, and visual localization. Achieving reliable correspondences under varying illumination, viewpoint changes, and occlusions remains a major challenge. This project proposes a method that leverages multi-modal sensor fusion and transformer-based architectures to improve robustness and accuracy.

    • Project No: 20204SUP
    • PI: Taha Koçyiğit
    • Funding: Boğaziçi University BAP
    • Start: 06.03.2025
    • Estimated End: 06.03.2026
    • Status: Ongoing

  • Segmentation Refinement with Large Vision Models

    We investigate how large vision models can be used to refine segmentation outputs, improving boundary quality, consistency across conditions, and robustness for real-world deployment.

  • On-Device LLM Assistant for Real-time & Dynamic API Execution

    (“Gerçek Zamanlı ve Dinamik API Yürütme için Cihaz Üzeri LLM Asistanı”)

    This project aims to develop an on-device large language model (LLM) assistant that can execute real-time, multi-step, and context-dependent API interactions on mobile devices. We will simulate multi-step and real-world scenarios where user requests may be incomplete or require situation-dependent context.

    • Project No: 24-50198
    • PI: Taha Koçyiğit
    • Funding: BAP – YÖK ADP
    • Start: 10.06.2025
    • Status: Ongoing

    Partnership: with Assoc. Prof. Şener Özönder and Turkcell Global Bilgi
    Program: FFPlus & EuroHPC (Digital Europe & Horizon Europe), 2025

  • EuroHPC Multi-Modal LLM for Cross-Modal Reasoning & Generation

    This work first targets generating large-scale synthetic multi-modal data and then adapting them into formats suitable as inputs for large language models (LLMs). This enables LLM reasoning and analysis capabilities to be applied over modalities such as infrared, radar, LiDAR, and millimeter-wave data.

    • Project No: EHPC-DEV-2025D06-088
    • PI: Taha Koçyiğit
    • Researchers: Suayb S. Arslan, Mehmet Turan
    • Funding: European Union
    • Start: 20.06.2025
    • Estimated End: 20.06.2026
    • Status: Ongoing

  • Revolutionizing Industrial Enzymes through Generative AI

    (“Üretken Yapay Zekâ ile Endüstriyel Enzim Devrimi”)

    In partnership with Asst. Prof. İbrahim Çağrı Kurt, this project explores how generative AI can accelerate the design and discovery process for industrial enzyme solutions.

    Funding: EuroHPC (Horizon Europe), 2025
